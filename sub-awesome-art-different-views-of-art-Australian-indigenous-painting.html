Space is represented in an entirely different way in the indigenous art of Australia. Australian indigenous peoples have a unique relationship to the land. Traditionally, they believe that they and all animals and plants belong to the land.

This painting is by Rover Thomas, who is widely regarded as one of the great contemporary Aboriginal painters of Australia. Rover Thomas had a traditional upbringing among his people in the East Kimberley region in Western Australia.

Despite their apparent simplicity, these red ochre paintings depict the East Kimberley landscape both as a map and as a spiritual place. They echo the colours of the desert lands and refer specifically to riverbeds, dirt tracks and homesteads. They also refer to sacred sites and even to places where there were massacres of his people by European settlers early last century.
