The Tonbak

The Tonbak, also called a Zarb, is a wooden goblet drum that has origins in the ancient Persian music of the Middle East. It has a single animal skin that is tuned by heating, cooling or applying moisture to it.

Tonbak players can make many different sounds on the skin by using different finger and hand strokes including clicking, tapping, beating and even changing the pitch by pressing with the fingers. Some people say that the Tonbak gets its name from the two main sounds played on the drum: ton in the middle, and bak on the edge.

The tonbak is often played in small Persian art music ensembles, which are common in Iran. It was originally used only as an accompanying instrument, but later become known as one of the worlds most intricate virtuosic drumming traditions.
