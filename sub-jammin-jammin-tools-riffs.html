Riffs

 

You've seen that scales can be turned into short phrases by playing groups of notes. If you take one of those phrases and play it over and over, it might start to become a riff.

A riff is a short, memorable phrase that can come from other solos and melodies that musicians have played. By learning and practising riffs, musicians have melody ideas that they can use in their improvised solos. They often find riffs they like when they are listening to songs. Then, they learn the notes and the rhythm they are played with and play those riffs in their own jams.

You can find riffs by listening to your favourite songs - they can be introductions, solos or the melody sung by the singer too. Or, you can make up your own out of scales and rhythmic patterns!
