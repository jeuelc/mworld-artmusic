Scale Types

 

The major scale, like the major chord, has a strong sound. It has seven notes in it and is also used to make lots of other scales. It can often be played with major chords. The notes in a major scale are also used to work out keys.

The minor scale also has seven notes and is similar to the major, but has a darker sound. There are a few different types including the natural and harmonic minor scales.

The pentatonic scale is similar to the natural minor, but two notes are left out. It's used a lot in rock and blues music, and is a favourite of guitar players.

The blues scale is the same as the minor pentatonic scale, with one extra note - the 'blue' note. This scale is used lots in jazz and blues jams, and works very well with those unfinished seventh chords.
