The Social Turn

 
click here

Many contemporary artists have made work that gets the audience to be part of the art. This development in art is called 'the social turn'. It comes from the idea that participation and communication between audience members is what is most important in art.

An important early example of this is the concept 'social sculpture' developed by the German artist Joseph Beuys. Beuys staged events in which audience members contributed to discussions and group activities designed to question how the world was being run.

Beuys was one of the founders of the Green Party in Germany, and there are now Green political parties all over the world. Beuys thought that human creativity was something that could be used to transform society and the world for the better.
