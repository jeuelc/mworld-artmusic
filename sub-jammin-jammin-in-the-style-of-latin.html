Latin

Lots of different styles of music have come from Latin America, including salsa from Cuba, bossa nova and samba from Brazil, and flamenco from Spain. These styles have one thing in common: a very strong rhythm. Most Latin styles are played around a rhythmic pattern called the 'clave' three long notes followed by two short ones. They are often very energetic and are played to make you dance!

Latin bands often feature instruments such as guitar, piano, trumpets and lots of percussion players, and feature a lot of jamming. Bands play melodies and rhythms that are quite complex, but are all connected to the clave pattern. Latin styles are so popular that lots of jazz and rock bands use these rhythms in their songs and jams.
