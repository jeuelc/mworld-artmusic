Involving the Audience

Art today is becoming more and more focused on involving the audience. This type of art is often called 'participatory art.' It is an approach to making art that relies on the audience to complete the artwork by experiencing it.

An example of this is walking into a piece of art, for example an artwork that is a very large room or space. Other examples include eating the art, for example art made by sweets, or assisting the artist in a piece of performance art, for example by staring at the artist!

It is important to the artist that the audience is engaged in the artwork directly, allowing them to become almost like co-authors of the work, as well as being observers of the work.
