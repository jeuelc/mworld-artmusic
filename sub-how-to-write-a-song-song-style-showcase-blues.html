Blues music evolved from African-American communities in the southern parts of the U.S.A. as a response to slavery and its aftermath. It traditionally was mournful and emotional, and provided a way to express the sorrows of a very hard life. The phrase 'the blues' has come to mean a feeling of sadness, but also a strong musical style. Some great blues artists are Muddy Waters (yes, that's really a person!), Robert Johnson and B. B. King.

Blues has a 'shuffle' rhythm, which feels a bit like a horse galloping, and gives the music a bouncing feel. It has its own special form the 12-bar blues. The most common blues songs have three lines of lyrics. The first two are almost the same, with slight melody changes to match the changing chords, and tell of a problem or sad event. The third line moves the story along by explaining the story more, or by finding a solution to the problem.
