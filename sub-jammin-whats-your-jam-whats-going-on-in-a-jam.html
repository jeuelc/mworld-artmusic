What's Going on in a Jam?

 
click here

Jams are often based on a set order of chords, called a chord progression. These give a basic structure to a jam. The chords in a progression often come from a bigger collection of notes, called a key. The key tells everybody which notes will sound like they belong with the music.

Musicians can take turns playing improvised solos, which are melodies made up of notes from the key. The other musicians can play chords and rhythms along with the soloing musician, helping their solo sound exciting and interesting!

A jam can be led by one person who might decide the chords, ask players to play a solo, or direct the band to get louder and softer, and stop playing. Some bands that jam together a lot can get to know each others playing so well that they interact and lead each other.
