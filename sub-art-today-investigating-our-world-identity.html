Many modern artists have used their art to explore issues of identity, to confront stereotypes, and to challenge traditional views of history.

For example, Cindy Sherman is a famous American photographer who has used her own image in her art to talk about how women are portrayed in the media. Her early works carefully restaged scenes from famous films, but in all cases she used herself as the model. Her work explores how people think about themselves, and questions how people are presented in and influenced by the media.

Contemporary indigenous artists in Australia have made work that confronts the history of European colonisation of Australia and the treatment of indigenous Australians. Australian artist Tracey Moffatt, for example, uses photography and film to create stories about the treatment of the indigenous population in Australia, as well as exploring the way the media has depicted the lives of women.
