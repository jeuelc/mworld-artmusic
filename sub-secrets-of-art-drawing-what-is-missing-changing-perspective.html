Changing Perspective

An obvious thing that is missing is the other side of the object. You have turned a three-dimensional object into a two-dimensional one and in the process you have forgotten about the other side. This is an inevitable result of using a fixed-point perspective.

So for a more complete drawing of your object, lets do a drawing that includes both sides of the object in the same drawing. A drawing with two perspectives, in other words. Cubism, an art movement of the early 20th century, attempted to account for multiple perspectives. Notice how the spaces in a cubist painting become fragmented as more and more fragments are added to the depiction of the objects.
