Now let's look at how space is explored by Chinese artists from the Song Dynasty. This was a high point in the history of Chinese painting.

These paintings have a 'universal perspective' with many points of detail woven in and out of the landscape. You get a sense of how important the landscape is - just as important as the people - to artists at this time. Song artists used fog or mist to create spaces of mystery and uncertainty. You have to imagine what these hidden parts of the landscape were like.

Artists from this period painted objects and people from their inner eye, their 'heart eye' as they called it, after studying them carefully. Often these paintings show a story where various stages are all shown in the one image.

They were often painted with transparent thin layers of ink called washes. And where is the viewpoint? Way up high in the sky where the artist could never have been, because aeroplanes weren't invented then!
