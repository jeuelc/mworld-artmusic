Some artists have devoted their working lives to drawing and painting negative spaces and other forms of 'space'. We can learn a lot about drawing from looking at their work.

The Italian artist Georgio Morandi was one such person. Morandi devoted years to drawing and painting a series of small objects over and over again. Look at the example of his work in the image cards. You can see that the spaces between the objects are emphasised just as much as the objects themselves. The whole image seems to jostle and vibrate.

The French artist Pierre Bonnard was fascinated by vision. He wanted his paintings to reveal themselves slowly and for the viewer to take time to work out what was happening in the scene.

Bonnard often painted scenes in his house that are confusing. The thing you normally look at first is at the edges of the painting and the things you don't normally notice are central. His paintings are designed for your eye to wander across them, before locking on to the key elements of the scene.
