The Cuban-born artist Felix Gonzalez-Torres made works for the audience to take or consume. His artwork 'Untitled (Placebo - Landscape - for Roni)' was a supply of sweets for the audience to take from the gallery for free. At the end of each day Gonzalez-Torres replenished the supply of sweets so that it weighed the same amount as it did at the start of the day.

The Argentinian-born artist Rirkrit Tiravanija prepares food for the audience of his work to eat. These works by Tiravanija and Gonzales-Torres challenge the idea that art is a commodity for sale.

For both these artists it is the social community that forms around eating that is the focus of the work. These occasions have the potential to generate new social possibilities - people meeting, conversations, chance connections and more - all within the gallery space.
