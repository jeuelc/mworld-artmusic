The Artist is Present

 

In 2010, Marina Abramovic performed a very unusual piece of performance art, at the Museum of Modern Art in New York. The work, called 'The Artist is Present', was a silent piece over 736 hours and 30 minutes, in which Abramovic sat immobile while spectators were invited to take turns sitting opposite her. Could you imagine doing something like this?

Sitting silently across from strangers one at a time for 736 hours sounds like a lot a whole lot! Why don't you try sitting motionless and silently across from each person at your home for a short period of time? You may get a glimpse of what Abramovic's work felt like. This really is an extreme example of performance art!
