Indonesian Gamelan

In Indonesia there are orchestras made up entirely of percussion instruments, called a Gamelan Orchestra. Gamelan is the main form of traditional music in Indonesia, and there are different types throughout the country. The two main types of gamelan are Javanese, from Java, and Balinese, from Bali.

In a gamelan orchestra musicians play a wide variety of tuned metal instruments and gongs, with interesting names like bonang, gender, slentham and kempul. The entire orchestra is led by a drummer playing the kendang, a two-headed drum, and the music is regularly punctuated by pounding big gongs!

Gamelan orchestras are often used to accompany wonderful theatre shows of shadow puppetry called wayang.
