West African Tribal Drumming

Throughout West Africa, which includes countries like Senegal, Ghana, Burkina Faso and Guinea, there are many cultures with tribal drumming groups.

Originally, tribal drumming was used in ancient ceremonies, sometimes for naming a child, or healing a sick person. In these traditions, lots of drummers play various repetitive interconnecting patterns. They use hand-carved drums made with animal skins, such as the djembe, dundun, bougarabou and kpanlogo.

Tribal ceremonial music is incredible to see performed, and people fly from all over the world today to watch and learn about traditional tribal drumming. It is so popular that there are classes and performers in many cities all over the world.
