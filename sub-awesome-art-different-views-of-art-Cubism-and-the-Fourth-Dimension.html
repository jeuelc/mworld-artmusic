Cubism and 'the Fourth Dimension'

 
click here click here click here

Two famous artists, Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque, were part of a movement that dramatically changed the way space was represented in art in the early 1900s.

Instead of painting from one single perspective, these artists wanted a more complete way to show places and things in time and space. They tried to paint an object from multiple viewpoints, including the fourth dimension - time!

The result was called 'Cubism' because of the many-faceted fragments that filled their canvases. Cubism was hugely influential. It led to many new approaches in art and architecture.
