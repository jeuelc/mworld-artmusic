Line Drawing

For this first drawing you will work only with lines. Don't confuse things with colour or shading. For now just use lines. Depending on how you use the pencil, how heavily you push or how lightly, lines can be delicate or rough and powerful. Lines are the starting point for almost all drawings.

Once you have worked out the position of the object on the page, begin to draw its outline lightly. Heavier lines can happen later. Draw the lines that travel across the object as well. Try to look for the basic shapes that make up the object and draw these.

Many of the things that surround us are made up of squares, circles, triangles, and rectangles. If you can see the object in terms of shapes its much easier! Sometimes the shapes are unusual ones. Remember, your line drawing is a record of your attempt to understand the object.
