Did you know that the oldest paintings found in caves date back to 30,000 or even 40,000 years ago!

Cave paintings have been found in many different parts of the world. In Australia cave paintings have been found that show images of megafauna that became extinct many thousands of years ago!

Cave paintings often show images of animals. Some historians think that the artists who made them were painting these images as part of sacred rituals, to communicate with animals and the spiritual world.

MEGAFAUNA

Megafauna means big animals - like really big beasts! Animals like wombats as big as a car, kangaroos as tall as a basketball ring, and massive, scary-looking birds! Megafauna lived in Australia until relatively recently. Most were extinct by 30,000 - 40,000 years ago. No one is quite sure why they went extinct.
