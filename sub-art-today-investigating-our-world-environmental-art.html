Environmental Art

 
click here

Lots of artists have made artwork about the environment in the twentieth century and beyond.

Some are concerned about the destruction of the natural world, or about global warming. Others are interested in the sense of beauty and joy they feel when they spend time in nature. Do you feel inspired when you're in a beautiful natural place like a forest, or beach, or desert? Maybe you could use these feelings to create art too.

Other artists have wanted to work outside, arguing that true art is already in the world, beyond the white cube of a gallery space.

A remarkable example of environmental art is 'The Lightning Field' by the American artist Walter De Maria. He put 400 stainless steel poles on a grid formation in the New Mexico desert. The work is huge! The poles are spread out over a large distance - one kilometre x one mile. But the work becomes dramatically bigger during thunderstorms because all the poles act as lightning conductors!

Walter De Maria wanted the people who visit the work to think about the invisible relationships between human beings and the wider universe.
