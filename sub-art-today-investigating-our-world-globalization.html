Globalisation

Our world is constantly changing. There's a never-ending flow of information, people and products around the globe. Borders between countries and cultures have opened up, changing and disrupting the lives of people everywhere. People call this globalisation.

The British artist Mike Nelson makes amazing installation art that explores these changes. In Nelson's case the viewer walks through the work and becomes absorbed and bewildered in its claustrophobic, dreamlike spaces that depict different cultures, places and beliefs.

In Nelson's extraordinary work 'I, Imposter', many rooms were built that made references to buildings in Istanbul, Turkey. The viewer has to do some detective work to figure out what each room refers to. What links all the different rooms is the search for something meaningful. It's an immersive, fascinating and confusing experience! It makes you think about the apparent distance between Eastern and Western cultures and how linked they are as well.

Installation Art

Installation art is work in which the artwork and the space it is in are seen as one entire thing. Installation art requires the viewer - that's you - to 'complete' the work in some way. Mike Nelson's artwork called 'I, Imposter,' which you can check out in the image cards, is an example of installation art.
